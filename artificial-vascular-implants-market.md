<p><p>Artificial vascular implants are classified into four types based on the material used: Expanded PolyTetraFluoroEthylene (EPTFE), Polyethylene Terephthalate, Polyurethane, and Others. EPTFE implants are known for their high biocompatibility and flexibility. Polyethylene Terephthalate implants are durable and have good mechanical strength. Polyurethane implants are flexible and resistant to wear. Other types of implants include materials such as silicone. Each type of implant offers unique characteristics and benefits for vascular surgeries, catering to the diverse needs of patients.</p></p>
