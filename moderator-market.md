<p><p>Graphite moderator is commonly used in high-temperature reactors due to its stability and strength. Heavy-water moderator, on the other hand, is preferred for pressurized heavy-water reactors as it effectively slows down neutrons without absorbing them. Other types of moderators include light water and beryllium, each with their unique properties and applications in nuclear reactors. The choice of moderator is crucial in determining the efficiency and safety of a reactor, as it directly impacts the neutron slowing-down process.</p></p>
