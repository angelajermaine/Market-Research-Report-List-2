<p><p>Physical hydrogen storage involves storing hydrogen gas in high-pressure tanks or as a cryogenic liquid. Chemical hydrogen storage involves converting hydrogen gas into another chemical compound that can later release the hydrogen when needed. Other markets in hydrogen storage technology include materials such as metal hydrides, carbon-based materials, and chemical sorbents that can adsorb and desorb hydrogen gas efficiently. Each type of storage technology has its own advantages and limitations, depending on factors such as cost, energy density, and safety considerations.</p></p>
