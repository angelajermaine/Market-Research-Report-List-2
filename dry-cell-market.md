<p><p>Primary cells are non-rechargeable cells that generate electric energy through a chemical reaction. They are widely used in household devices like remote controls. Secondary cells, on the other hand, are rechargeable cells that can be reused multiple times by applying an electric current to reverse the chemical reaction. They are commonly found in electronic devices like smartphones and laptops. Both primary and secondary cells cater to different market needs and applications.</p></p>
