<p><p>Darjeeling tea is categorized into different market types based on the size and quality of the tea leaves. Whole tea leaves are considered the highest quality, with intact leaves that provide a full-bodied flavor. Broken leaves are smaller pieces but still retain good quality and flavor. Fannings are even smaller fragments used in tea bags, while dust is the smallest and lowest quality grade often used in mass-produced tea bags. Each market type offers a different taste and quality experience for consumers.</p></p>
