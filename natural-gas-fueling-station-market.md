<p><p>CNG fueling stations refer to compressed natural gas stations that compress natural gas to a pressure of 3,600 psi for vehicle refueling. These stations are suitable for smaller-scale operations due to their lower energy density. On the other hand, LNG fueling stations liquefy natural gas for vehicle refueling, offering a higher energy density and are typically used for larger-scale operations. Both types of fueling stations offer environmentally-friendly alternatives to traditional gasoline and diesel fuels.</p></p>
