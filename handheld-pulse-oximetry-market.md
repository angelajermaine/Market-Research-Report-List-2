<p><p>Handheld pulse oximetry is a non-invasive medical device used to measure oxygen saturation levels in the blood. It is a portable device that typically clips onto a patient's finger, earlobe, or toe to provide real-time monitoring of oxygen levels. Handheld pulse oximeters are widely used in hospitals, clinics, and home health care settings to assess respiratory function and monitor patients with respiratory conditions such as asthma, COPD, and sleep apnea.</p><p>The handheld pulse oximetry market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.2% during the forecast period. The growth of the market is driven by factors such as increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases, rising geriatric population, technological advancements in pulse oximetry devices, and growing demand for portable and easy-to-use monitoring devices. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has further boosted the demand for handheld pulse oximeters, as they are essential tools in monitoring oxygen levels in patients with the virus.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the handheld pulse oximetry market include the development of wireless and wearable pulse oximeters, integration of data analytics and cloud connectivity, and increasing focus on telemedicine and remote patient monitoring.
