<p><p>Synthetic vascular prostheses are artificial blood vessels used in vascular surgeries to replace or repair damaged vessels. The market is segmented into ePTFE vascular grafts, polyester vascular grafts, PTFE vascular grafts, and others. ePTFE grafts are made from expanded polytetrafluoroethylene, providing good biocompatibility and durability. Polyester grafts are made from polyester fibers, offering flexibility and strength. PTFE grafts are made from polytetrafluoroethylene, commonly known as Teflon, known for its non-stick properties. Other types of grafts include hybrid and bioactive grafts, each offering unique benefits for different patient needs.</p></p>
