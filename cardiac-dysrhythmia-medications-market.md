<p><p>Cardiac dysrhythmia medications can be categorized into two main types: oral antiarrhythmic medications and intravenous antiarrhythmic medications. Oral antiarrhythmic medications are taken by mouth and are used for long-term management of cardiac dysrhythmias. They work by regulating the electrical impulses in the heart to restore normal rhythm. In contrast, intravenous antiarrhythmic medications are administered through a vein and are used for immediate treatment of severe or life-threatening dysrhythmias. Both types of medications play a crucial role in managing and treating cardiac dysrhythmias.</p></p>
